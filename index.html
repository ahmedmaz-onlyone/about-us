AL-ARISH PALM SHELTER

Sustainable Housing for Arid Areas

About the Team

We are a team of Grade 10 STEM students who developed an eco-friendly housing solution using palm fronds to address climate change, extreme heat, and flash floods in Al-Arish, Egypt.

The project is designed to address challenges in arid areas such as extreme heat, flash floods, sandstorms, and poor thermal insulation. Al-Arish was selected due to its desert climate, proximity to Wadi Al-Arish, and the availability of palm fronds as a local sustainable material.

Project Objectives

  • Use eco-friendly and locally available materials
  • Improve thermal insulation in desert housing
  • Resist fire, wind, and flash floods
  • Provide a low-cost solution for low-income residents
